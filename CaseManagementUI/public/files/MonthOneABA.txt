Month 1 ABA Session Plan – 7-Year-Old (In-Home)
Goals for Month 1

Build rapport (pairing with reinforcement, child comfort).

Conduct preference assessments.

Collect baseline data (language, play, social, daily living, behavior).

Introduce structure (visual schedule, token system).

Begin 2–3 individualized skill targets (small, achievable wins).

Session Structure (Sample 2-Hour Block)
0 – 10 min | Warm-Up & Pairing

Greeting & rapport-building (play with preferred toys, join ongoing activity).

Review visual schedule/token board with child.

Caregiver check-in.

10 – 30 min | Preference Assessment & Free Play

Conduct paired-choice or free operant preference assessment.

Join child-led play, narrate actions, model social language.

30 – 60 min | Baseline Skill Assessment

Brief probes in:

Language (manding, labeling, conversational turn-taking).

Daily Living (dressing, hygiene routines).

Cognition/Academics (matching, counting, reading comprehension).

Play/Social (cooperative play, flexibility).

Collect ABC data if problem behavior occurs.

60 – 75 min | Movement / Break

Gross motor play (ball toss, obstacle course, trampoline).

Incorporate compliance and transitions back to table work.

75 – 105 min | Skill Acquisition Block

Introduce 2–3 skill programs (examples for Month 1):

Functional Communication Training (FCT): requesting help or items.

Daily Living: brushing teeth step, tying shoes, setting table.

Social: greeting script, sharing turn in a game.

Use DTT (Discrete Trial Training) or NET (Natural Environment Teaching) depending on target.

105 – 115 min | Behavior Plan Practice

Review any maladaptive behaviors (aggression, elopement, tantrums).

Practice replacement behaviors (asking for break, using coping card).

115 – 120 min | Wrap-Up

End with a preferred activity.

Review session data with caregiver.

Assign home practice (1–2 easy targets for parent to support).

Caregiver Involvement in Month 1

Session debriefs at end.

Introduce basic ABA principles (reinforcement, prompting, shaping).

Train parent on 1 simple strategy each week (e.g., giving clear instructions, token economy).
