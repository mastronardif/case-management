0 – 10 min → Warm-Up & Pairing

Greet child and caregiver warmly.

Build rapport (play with a preferred toy, join child’s activity).

Take quick notes from caregiver about day/mood/events.

Set out token board or reinforcement system.

10 – 40 min → Skill Acquisition Block 1

Discrete Trial Training (DTT) or Natural Environment Teaching (NET) depending on program goals.

Example goals:

Requesting (mands) – “I want ___.”

Receptive language – following 1-2 step instructions.

Fine motor imitation (clap hands, stack blocks).

Use prompting hierarchy (most-to-least or least-to-most).

Reinforce successes immediately.

Collect data on each trial.

40 – 50 min → Movement / Sensory Break

Gross motor play (jumping, obstacle course, yoga poses).

Use as reinforcement and to reset attention.

50 – 80 min → Skill Acquisition Block 2

Rotate into different goal areas:

Daily living skills (washing hands, brushing teeth, putting on shoes).

Social skills (turn-taking game, greetings, sharing).

Cognitive/pre-academic (colors, numbers, matching).

Continue data collection.

80 – 95 min → Behavior Management / Problem Behavior Protocol

Run behavior intervention plan (BIP) if targeted behaviors arise.

Practice replacement behaviors (functional communication, coping strategies).

Reinforce alternative behaviors.

Track frequency/duration of behaviors.

95 – 110 min → Parent / Caregiver Training & Involvement

Model strategies used during session.

Coach caregiver to run 1–2 targets.

Provide feedback in real time.

110 – 120 min → Cool-Down & Session Wrap-Up

Transition to a preferred activity for the child.

Review session data with caregiver (briefly).

Highlight wins (“He used 3 new words today!”).

Preview next session focus.
