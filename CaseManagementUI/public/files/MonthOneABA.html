Month 1 — ABA Treatment Plan (Age 7)

Focused on assessment, rapport, establishing routines, and initial skill acquisition. Use this as a master plan for the first 4 weeks of services.

Month Goals (Top 5)

  1. Establish strong rapport & pairing with therapist and reinforcers.
  2. Complete preference assessments & collect baseline data.
  3. Introduce visual schedule & token economy for transitions.
  4. Begin 2–3 foundational programs (functional communication, daily living, one academic goal).
  5. Start caregiver coaching: 1 brief strategy/week.

Weekly Focus (Overview)

WeekFocusKey Activities
Week 1 Pairing, Preference, Baseline Play-based pairing, free-operant & paired-choice preference assessment, brief probes on language & behavior baseline.
Week 2 Structure & Token System Introduce visual schedule, token board; train compliance to simple directives; begin discrete trials for 1–2 targets.
Week 3 Skill Acquisition Start systematic DTT/NET for functional communication (requesting), daily living step, and one academic task (matching/reading comprehension).
Week 4 Caregiver Coaching & Generalization Coach caregiver to run targets, practice generalization in new settings at home, collect progress data and revise goals.

Suggested Programs to Start (Examples)

  • Functional Communication Training (FCT): request break, help, or item using picture or phrase.
  • Daily Living: 1-step to 3-step tasks (e.g., handwashing sequence, putting on jacket).
  • Academic/Pre-Academic: matching, reading comprehension questions (short passages), math facts (counting/facts practice).
  • Social Skills: greeting, turn-taking, joint attention games.

Behavior & Safety

Implement existing BIP if behaviors occur. During Month 1 focus heavily on prevention (environmental arrangement, reinforcement schedules, clear transitions). Use ABC notes for incidents.

Quick Reference: Caregiver Coaching Topics (Week-by-week)
  1. Week 1 — Reinforcement basics & following simple instructions.
  2. Week 2 — Using the token board and consistent praise.
  3. Week 3 — Encouraging communication & prompting hierarchy.
  4. Week 4 — Generalization: practicing targets during daily routines.

Measurement Plan

Aim for objective, repeatable measures: trials & corrects for DTT targets, frequency/duration for behaviors, percent independent for daily living tasks.

Caregiver Handout — How to Support Between Sessions

  • Keep it short: 2–5 minutes, 2–3 times daily on assigned targets.
  • Use the token board: give tokens for attempts, exchange for preferred activity.
  • Be consistent with language: simple, direct instructions; praise effort specifically.
  • Record briefly: Note 1 successful attempt per day — it helps the BCBA see progress.

Tip: prioritize caregiver confidence over perfection. Small, consistent practice yields results.
